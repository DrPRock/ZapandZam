Aquamarine and Bloodstone: March's Birthstones

Aquamarine

Aquamarine is the birthstone for March and is a variety of the mineral beryl. Its name comes from the Latin word "aqua marina," which means "water of the sea." The stone is known for its pale blue or greenish-blue color, which is reminiscent of the ocean.

Aquamarine has been used in jewelry for centuries and is said to have a calming effect on its wearer. It is also believed to promote courage and communication, making it a popular choice for engagement rings.

Some of the most famous aquamarines include the Dom Pedro aquamarine, which weighs 10,363 carats, and the Martha Rocha aquamarine, which was named after the famous Brazilian beauty queen who won the Miss Universe contest in 1954.

Properties and Meaning of Aquamarine

Aquamarine is a relatively hard gemstone, ranking at 7.5 to 8 on the Mohs scale of hardness. It is a transparent stone with a vitreous luster and is often cut into faceted gemstones. The color of aquamarine is due to the presence of iron in the crystal structure.

In addition to its calming and communicative properties, aquamarine is also thought to have healing properties. It is said to help with respiratory and throat problems, as well as with allergies and autoimmune disorders. It is also believed to promote emotional stability and self-awareness.

Bloodstone

Today, bloodstone is still considered a powerful healing stone. It is believed to help with blood disorders, including anemia, as well as with the immune system and detoxification. It is also said to promote courage and creativity.
